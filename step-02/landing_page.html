What do I like?

What Music do I like?

I've always prided myself on having a diverse music taste. I listen to everything from metal to ambient music to classical to jazz to blues. In my early teens I really got into metal music from bands like Metallica and Pantera. Next thing you know I'm listening to bands like ERRA and Invent Animate. I've always been a really been fan of progressive music, or "prog" as the scene likes to call it. And from listening to bands and artists like Plini, Arch Echo, and Animals as Leaders I took an interest in other genres. I started listening to more jazz fusion from artists like Greg Howe, Allan Holdsworth, Gnarbot, and Eric Johnson. From there on I started listening to more jazz, getting reccomendations from friends and researching the influences of my favorite artists. This led me to develop a deep love and appreciation for the genre of Jazz. This love of jazz has genres I would have never known about, such as city pop, my latest kick. I've been a big fan of anime from a while and in turn japanese music. I've always noticed that japanese music, for the most part, was generally a lot more melodic than it's american counter parts. After listening to some interviews and doing some research I had found out that the developing Japanese music scene was heavily inspired by the early American jazz scene. But unlike in america where the jazz influence in pop music eventually died off, it remained the foundation of modern Japanese music. All of that to say I have a great love and appreciation for Jazz and what it's done for modern music as a whole. Some of my favorite music is what you would call progressive metal, djent, or thall. These subgenres of metal are generally quite rhythmically technical such as the music of Meshuggah, which are arguably one of if not the most influential bands in metal.


What are my favorite albums?

Now for a list of some of my current favorite albums from a few different genres!



Mint Jams by Casiopea has been on repeat for me ever since I heard it. This is their live album from 1982 performance at tokyo hall. The music they play is a beautiful blend of jazz, funk, instrumental r&b, and city pop. If you are to listen to any songs from this album I would reccomend the first two tracks "Take me" and "Asayake". This is also one of if not the best sounding live albums I've ever heard.

Next up on our list is one of the greatest metal albums of all time, M책sstaden under vatten by Vildhjarta. This 17 track concept album, over 8 years in the making has firmly planted a spot in my favorite albums of all time. This is the sequel to the previous concept album "M책sstaden." This album is full polyrhythms, crushing guitars, melancholic ambience, obsenely low tunings, and oustanding production. My favorite tracks have to the "Mitt trotta hjarta", "Paradiso", and "Br채nnm채rkt".

Timely!!! by Anri has to be my favorite city pop album. If you are not familiar with city pop, city pop is the name for the style of music from the 70's and 80's in Japan that was pop music with a very heavy jazz influence. This album is less jazzy than one of the later entries in this list but still a fantastic album. My favorite track is "Driving my Love". This is not only my favorite city pop album but also my favorite pop album in general. It's just really great easy listening music that I think any musician or jazz lover can appreciate.

There's not much to say about Heavner by Invent Animate. it's a 10/10 album and is one of the best metal releases of all time. A fanastic blend of ambience, crushing riffs, catchy choruses, and emotion. My favorite tracks are "Shade Astray", "Purity Weeps", "Emberglow" and "Elysium".

ERRA's self titled album, specifically the deluxe edition is oustanding. Same story when it comes to Heavener, just an oustanding album and one of the best metal releases of all time, in my opinion that is. It's a lot more progressive than some other albums on this list. My favorite song on the album is probably the most "out there" song which would be "Lunar Halo". Starting a metal song off with an atmospheric solo vocoder section in mixolydian is one of the boldest things you can do in metal right now to say the least. Just an oustanding album. My favorite tracks are "Lunar Halo", "House of Glass", "Vanish Canvas", and "Gungrave".

Sunshower by Taeko Onuki has been one of my favorite albums to listen to as of late. It's a really great album that is more parts jazz than it is city pop. Some songs feel like it is just a jazz song with female vocals over it. My favorite song is easily "Kusuri o Takusan"

And to now close the list off with I let it in and it took everything by Loathe Just an overall oustanding album and one of the most unique sounding albums in metal. It's a great mix of alt rock, shoe gaze, ambience, and some of the heaviest music in all of metal. I almost can't pick a favorite off this album but if I had to it would be "I let it in and it took everything" and "Is it really you?"
